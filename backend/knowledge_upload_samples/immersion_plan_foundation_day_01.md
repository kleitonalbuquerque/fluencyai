# FluencyAI Knowledge Seed - Immersion Plan Day 01

## Purpose

This document is a structured source for FluencyAI to generate a daily immersion plan for beginner to lower-intermediate English learners. It should support weekly roadmap generation, vocabulary practice, phrase memorization, grammar explanation, quiz creation, role play prompts, and AI conversation feedback.

## Learner Profile

- Native language: Brazilian Portuguese
- Target language: English
- Level: A1 to A2
- Main objective: speak with confidence in daily situations
- Tone: friendly, direct, encouraging, and practical
- Focus: travel, work introductions, daily routines, and small talk

## Weekly Roadmap Theme

Week 1 theme: essential daily communication.

The weekly roadmap should help the learner:

- introduce themselves
- talk about routines
- ask simple questions
- order food or drinks
- ask for directions
- describe preferences
- review vocabulary with spaced repetition

## Day 01 Lesson Goal

Day 01 should focus on self-introduction and basic daily conversation.

The learner should be able to:

- say their name and where they are from
- describe what they do
- say why they are learning English
- ask the other person simple personal questions
- respond politely when they do not understand

## Grammar Focus

### 1. Verb "to be" for identity and origin

Use "am", "are", and "is" to talk about identity, origin, and basic descriptions.

Examples:

- I am Ana.
- I am from Brazil.
- You are a student.
- He is from Canada.

Common learner issue:

- Incorrect: I from Brazil.
- Correct: I am from Brazil.

### 2. Simple Present for routines and facts

Use the simple present to talk about routines, facts, and regular actions.

Examples:

- I study English every day.
- I work in technology.
- I live in Sao Paulo.
- I practice speaking at night.

Common learner issue:

- Incorrect: I studying English every day.
- Correct: I study English every day.

### 3. Questions with "what", "where", and "why"

Use question words to ask for personal information.

Examples:

- What is your name?
- Where are you from?
- What do you do?
- Why are you learning English?

Common learner issue:

- Incorrect: Where you are from?
- Correct: Where are you from?

### 4. Polite requests with "could you"

Use "Could you..." to ask politely.

Examples:

- Could you repeat that?
- Could you speak slowly, please?
- Could you help me?

Common learner issue:

- Incorrect: Repeat, please.
- Better: Could you repeat that, please?

## Pronunciation Notes

- "Name" has one syllable: /neim/.
- "Live" in "I live in Sao Paulo" sounds like /liv/, not /laiv/.
- "Work" has the sound /w/ at the beginning. Round your lips slightly.
- "Question" sounds like /kwes-chun/.
- "Comfortable speed" matters more than perfect accent.

## Speaking Exercise

Ask the learner to speak for 45 to 60 seconds using this structure:

1. Say your name.
2. Say where you are from.
3. Say where you live.
4. Say what you do.
5. Say why you are learning English.
6. Ask one question back.

Expected learner answer:

"My name is Ana. I am from Brazil. I live in Sao Paulo. I work in technology. I study English every day because I want to speak with more confidence. What is your name?"

## Role Play Scenario

Scenario: meeting a coworker for the first time.

AI first prompt:

"Hi, nice to meet you. My name is Sarah. What is your name?"

Expected learner actions:

- respond with their name
- say where they are from
- mention their work or studies
- ask one question back

Correction style:

- correct only the most important mistake first
- explain briefly in Portuguese
- give one improved version in English
- ask a natural follow-up question

## Quiz Questions

Use these questions to generate a short quiz.

1. Which sentence is correct?
   - A. I from Brazil.
   - B. I am from Brazil.
   - C. I are from Brazil.
   - Answer: B

2. What does "Could you repeat that?" mean?
   - A. Voce poderia repetir isso?
   - B. Voce quer cafe?
   - C. Onde voce mora?
   - Answer: A

3. Complete: I ___ English every day.
   - A. studies
   - B. study
   - C. studying
   - Answer: B

4. Which question asks about origin?
   - A. What do you do?
   - B. Where are you from?
   - C. Why are you learning English?
   - Answer: B

5. Choose the polite request:
   - A. Repeat now.
   - B. Could you speak slowly, please?
   - C. You speak slowly.
   - Answer: B

## AI Feedback Rules

When correcting the learner:

- Keep the tone friendly.
- Do not overcorrect every sentence.
- Prefer one clear correction and one natural example.
- Explain grammar in Portuguese when the learner is A1 or A2.
- Encourage the learner to answer again with the improved sentence.

Example correction:

"Isso soa bem. Uma melhoria pequena: em ingles, para dizer origem, use 'I am from Brazil', nao apenas 'I from Brazil'. Tente assim: 'I am from Brazil, and I live in Sao Paulo.'"

## Weekly Roadmap Output Expectations

When this document is used to create a weekly roadmap, the roadmap should include:

- a daily objective
- 20 essential phrases
- 15 to 20 vocabulary words
- 3 to 5 grammar points
- one speaking task
- one quiz
- one review activity
- one role play prompt

The plan should be practical, not theoretical. Every activity should help the learner speak or understand real English.
